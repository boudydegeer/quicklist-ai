How Amazon's A9 Algorithm Actually Works in 2026

Amazon's search algorithm (A9, now sometimes called COSMO) determines which products appear when a buyer searches. Understanding it is the difference between page 1 and page 10.

A9 works in two phases: relevance matching (does your listing contain the search terms?) and performance ranking (does your listing convert when shown?). You need both to succeed on Amazon.

Relevance Signals

  • Product title — Most heavily weighted. Amazon indexes every word in your title for search matching.
  • Bullet points — Indexed and weighted, but less than titles. Each bullet should contain at least one keyword phrase.
  • Backend search terms — 250 bytes of hidden keywords. This is where you put synonyms, alternate spellings, and Spanish terms you can't fit in visible fields.
  • Product description / A+ Content — Indexed but given the least keyword weight. Focus on conversion here, not SEO.

Performance Signals

  • Click-through rate (CTR) from search results
  • Conversion rate once on the listing page
  • Sales velocity relative to competitors
  • Review count and rating
  • Price competitiveness
  • Fulfillment method — FBA listings get a ranking boost over FBM

The Anatomy of a High-Converting Amazon Title

Amazon allows up to 200 characters for most categories, but the optimal title length is 80-150 characters. Here's the proven structure:

[Brand] [Primary Keyword] — [Key Feature], [Material/Size], [Use Case] ([Quantity/Pack])

Each element serves a purpose: Brand builds trust, the primary keyword drives relevance, and the remaining elements improve click-through rate by answering buyer questions before they click.

Bullet Point Optimization: Benefits Over Features

Amazon gives you 5 bullet points, each up to 500 characters. Most sellers waste them with flat feature lists. High-converting bullets follow this formula:

BENEFIT IN CAPS — Supporting details that expand on the benefit, include a keyword, and address a buyer concern.

  • Lead each bullet with the benefit, not the feature
  • Include at least one keyword phrase per bullet (different keyword each time)
  • Address common objections or questions ("Is it dishwasher safe?" → include that detail)
  • Use all 5 bullets — empty bullets are lost ranking opportunities
  • Stay under 250 characters per bullet for mobile readability

Backend Keywords: The Hidden Ranking Booster

Amazon gives you 250 bytes of backend search terms that buyers never see. This is arguably the most underutilized field in Amazon SEO. Use it for:

  • Synonyms: If your title says "water bottle," your backend can include "flask," "thermos," "tumbler"
  • Alternate spellings: "grey" vs "gray," common misspellings of your product type
  • Spanish terms: Amazon indexes Spanish search terms — include translations of your main keywords
  • Long-tail phrases: "best water bottle for hiking," "insulated bottle for gym"
  • Complementary products: Items often purchased together or searched in the same context

Rules: Don't repeat words already in your title or bullets. Don't use commas (spaces only). Don't include brand names, ASINs, or subjective claims ("best," "cheapest").

Category-Specific Amazon Tips

Amazon's A9 algorithm weights different factors by category. FBA sellers should know:

  • Health & Beauty: Ingredient keywords are heavily searched. Include active ingredients in title and bullets.
  • Electronics: Compatibility keywords matter. "Compatible with iPhone 15" is a high-volume search term.
  • Home & Kitchen: Dimensions and material keywords drive search traffic. "BPA-free" and "dishwasher safe" are must-haves.
  • Toys & Games: Age range keywords are critical. "Toys for 3 year old boys" gets massive search volume.
