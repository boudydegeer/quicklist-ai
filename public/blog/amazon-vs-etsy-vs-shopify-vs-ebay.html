Amazon vs Etsy vs Shopify vs eBay: Where to Sell Online in 2026

By QuickList AI Team · March 2026
TL;DR: Each marketplace has distinct strengths. Amazon dominates for volume and brand-name products, Etsy is unbeatable for handmade and vintage items, Shopify gives you full brand control with zero marketplace fees, and eBay excels for used goods, collectibles, and auctions. Most successful sellers in 2026 sell on multiple platforms. QuickList AI helps you optimize listings for all four.

If you're starting an e-commerce business in 2026 — or looking to expand an existing one — the first major decision is where to sell online. The four biggest options are Amazon, Etsy, Shopify, and eBay. Each platform serves different seller types, charges different fees, and attracts a different audience.

In this comprehensive guide, we break down the Amazon vs Etsy vs Shopify vs eBay comparison across every dimension that matters: fees, audience, competition, ease of use, product fit, and SEO. By the end, you'll know exactly which marketplace (or combination) is right for your business.

1. Amazon: The Volume Powerhouse

Amazon remains the single largest e-commerce marketplace on the planet in 2026, with over 310 million active customer accounts and nearly 40% of all US online retail sales. If you want sheer volume and are willing to compete on price and reviews, Amazon is hard to beat.

Amazon Fees

  • Individual plan: $0.99 per item sold (no monthly fee)
  • Professional plan: $39.99/month (unlimited listings)
  • Referral fee: 8%–15% per sale depending on category (most categories are 15%)
  • FBA fees: Additional fulfillment fees if you use Fulfillment by Amazon (starting around $3.22 per unit for standard-size items)

Audience and Demographics

Amazon shoppers are intent-driven buyers. They arrive with a product in mind and are ready to purchase. The demographic skews broad — virtually every age group and income bracket shops on Amazon. Over 200 million Amazon Prime members expect fast, free shipping, which means FBA sellers have a significant advantage.

Competition Level

Competition on Amazon is extremely high. There are over 2 million active third-party sellers, and you're often competing directly against Amazon's own private-label brands. Winning the Buy Box is critical, and it typically requires competitive pricing, strong reviews, and excellent fulfillment metrics.

Ease of Use

Setting up an Amazon seller account is straightforward, but mastering the platform takes time. Product listings must follow strict guidelines, and optimizing for Amazon's A9 search algorithm requires specific keyword strategies. Expect 1–2 weeks to get fully up and running with your first optimized listings.

Best Product Types

  • Brand-name and commodity products
  • Private-label products with strong differentiation
  • Products with high search volume (electronics, home goods, beauty)
  • Wholesale and arbitrage products

SEO and Discoverability

Amazon has its own internal search engine. Listings are ranked based on relevance, conversion rate, and sales velocity. You have limited control over external SEO — Amazon product pages do rank on Google, but you can't customize meta tags or URL structures. Your product title, bullet points, and backend keywords are what matter most.

2. Etsy: The Handmade and Vintage Specialist

Etsy has carved out a powerful niche as the go-to marketplace for handmade, vintage, and unique items. With over 95 million active buyers in 2026 and a loyal community that values craftsmanship, Etsy is ideal for creative entrepreneurs and artisans.

Etsy Fees

  • Listing fee: $0.20 per listing (lasts 4 months or until sold)
  • Transaction fee: 6.5% of the sale price (including shipping)
  • Payment processing: 3% + $0.25 per transaction
  • Etsy Plus (optional): $10/month for shop customization and credits
  • Offsite Ads fee: 12%–15% on sales generated from Etsy's advertising (mandatory for shops earning over $10K/year)

Audience and Demographics

Etsy shoppers tend to be female, aged 25–44, and willing to pay a premium for unique, handmade, or personalized products. They're often looking for gifts, home decor, jewelry, wedding items, and one-of-a-kind goods. The Etsy buyer values the story behind the product almost as much as the product itself.

Competition Level

Competition on Etsy is moderate and growing. There are over 7 million active sellers, but the niche nature of the platform means you can differentiate through craftsmanship, photography, and brand storytelling. The rise of print-on-demand and digital products has increased competition in those categories specifically.

Ease of Use

Etsy is one of the easiest platforms to start selling on. You can have a shop live within a few hours. The listing process is intuitive, and Etsy provides built-in tools for shop analytics, shipping labels, and customer messaging. Setup time is typically under a day.

Best Product Types

  • Handmade goods (jewelry, clothing, ceramics, art)
  • Vintage items (20+ years old)
  • Digital downloads (planners, printables, SVG files)
  • Personalized and custom products
  • Craft supplies

SEO and Discoverability

Etsy SEO is a blend of on-platform and Google search optimization. Etsy listings frequently rank well on Google, especially for long-tail queries. You have control over titles, tags (13 per listing), categories, and attributes. Etsy's search algorithm weighs relevance, listing quality score, recency, and shop history. A solid Etsy SEO strategy can drive significant organic traffic.

3. Shopify: Full Brand Control

Shopify isn't a marketplace — it's a standalone e-commerce platform that lets you build your own online store. With over 4.6 million active stores in 2026, Shopify powers everything from small side hustles to billion-dollar brands like Gymshark and Allbirds.

Shopify Fees

  • Basic plan: $39/month
  • Shopify plan: $105/month
  • Advanced plan: $399/month
  • Transaction fee: 0% if using Shopify Payments; 0.5%–2% otherwise
  • Credit card rates: 2.9% + $0.30 (Basic) down to 2.4% + $0.30 (Advanced)
  • No listing fees or marketplace commissions

Audience and Demographics

Here's the key difference: Shopify doesn't come with a built-in audience. You need to drive your own traffic through social media, paid ads, content marketing, email, or SEO. The upside is that you own the customer relationship entirely — their email, their data, their lifetime value. Demographics depend entirely on your brand and marketing strategy.

Competition Level

Competition exists but it's indirect. You're not competing on a shared marketplace page. Instead, you're competing for attention across the open web. This means your success depends more on your marketing skills, brand strength, and ability to drive traffic than on competing with other sellers on a search results page.

Ease of Use

Shopify is beginner-friendly for store setup with drag-and-drop themes and an intuitive admin panel. However, building a complete, conversion-optimized store takes more effort than listing on a marketplace. Expect 1–4 weeks to launch a polished Shopify store with proper product descriptions and SEO.

Best Product Types

  • Branded products with a unique value proposition
  • Products with strong visual appeal (fashion, beauty, lifestyle)
  • Subscription products
  • High-margin products that justify ad spend
  • Products with a strong social media following or influencer strategy

SEO and Discoverability

Shopify gives you full control over SEO: custom meta titles, descriptions, URL slugs, blog content, structured data, sitemap, and more. You can build a content-driven SEO strategy with Shopify's built-in blog. The downside is that you're starting from zero domain authority, whereas marketplace listings benefit from the platform's existing authority.

4. eBay: The Marketplace for Everything

eBay has reinvented itself over the past few years and remains a strong platform for used goods, collectibles, refurbished items, and niche products. With 134 million active buyers globally in 2026, eBay offers a unique combination of auction-style and fixed-price selling.

eBay Fees

  • Listing fee: First 250 listings/month are free; $0.35 per listing after that
  • Final value fee: 10%–15% of the total sale amount (varies by category)
  • Payment processing: Included in the final value fee (managed payments)
  • eBay Store subscriptions: $4.95/month (Starter) to $2,999.95/month (Anchor), which increase free listing allotments and reduce fees

Audience and Demographics

eBay shoppers are deal-seekers and collectors. The audience skews slightly older (35–55) and is more male-dominated than Etsy. eBay buyers are comfortable purchasing used, refurbished, and open-box items. They're also highly engaged in niche categories like trading cards, auto parts, electronics, and vintage goods.

Competition Level

Competition on eBay is moderate. While there are millions of sellers, the diversity of product conditions (new, used, refurbished) and selling formats (auction, Buy It Now) creates natural segmentation. You can find profitable niches more easily than on Amazon, especially in used and vintage categories.

Ease of Use

eBay has significantly improved its seller experience in recent years. Listing creation is straightforward, with barcode scanning for quick product identification. The mobile app makes it easy to photograph and list items on the go. Setup time is minimal — you can be selling within hours.

Best Product Types

  • Used and refurbished electronics
  • Collectibles (cards, coins, memorabilia)
  • Auto parts and accessories
  • Vintage clothing and goods
  • Overstock and liquidation inventory
  • Niche hobby products

SEO and Discoverability

eBay listings get strong indexing on Google, especially for long-tail product searches and specific model numbers. eBay's internal search engine (Cassini) prioritizes item specifics, seller performance, and listing quality. You have moderate SEO control through titles, item specifics, and descriptions, but no custom URL structures.

Which Marketplace Is Right for You?

There's no single "best" marketplace. The right choice depends on what you sell, your budget, and your growth goals. Here's a quick decision framework:

Choose Amazon if...

  • You sell brand-name or commodity products with proven demand
  • You want access to the largest buyer pool with high purchase intent
  • You're willing to invest in FBA and compete on price and reviews
  • You have the budget for PPC advertising on the platform
  • You prioritize sales volume over brand building

Choose Etsy if...

  • You create handmade, vintage, or personalized products
  • Your products have a unique story or creative angle
  • You want an easy, low-cost way to start selling online
  • You're comfortable with Etsy's community guidelines and niche focus
  • You sell digital downloads like printables, templates, or SVG files

Choose Shopify if...

  • You want to build a long-term brand with full creative control
  • You have a marketing strategy for driving your own traffic (social, ads, content)
  • You sell products with strong margins that justify customer acquisition costs
  • You want to own the customer relationship and data
  • You plan to scale into a multi-channel DTC brand

Choose eBay if...

  • You sell used, refurbished, or second-hand products
  • You're in collectibles, auto parts, electronics, or niche hobbies
  • You want flexible selling formats (auction + fixed price)
  • You have liquidation, overstock, or discontinued inventory
  • You want a quick and easy way to monetize items you already own

Consider multi-marketplace selling if...

  • You want to maximize reach and reduce dependency on a single platform
  • Your products appeal to different buyer personas across platforms
  • You have the operational capacity to manage inventory and orders across channels
  • You want to test which platform converts best for your specific products
  • You're serious about building a sustainable e-commerce business in 2026

Multi-marketplace selling is the strategy used by the most successful e-commerce businesses. The challenge is keeping your listings optimized across platforms with different algorithms, keyword strategies, and content requirements. That's exactly the problem multi-marketplace optimization tools are designed to solve.

The Hidden Cost: Listing Optimization Across Platforms

One thing most sellers underestimate is the time and effort required to optimize listings differently for each platform. What works on Amazon (keyword-stuffed bullet points) doesn't work on Etsy (story-driven descriptions). What ranks on eBay (item specifics) is irrelevant on Shopify (meta descriptions and blog content).

Each platform has its own search algorithm, its own content best practices, and its own character limits. Manually optimizing a single product for all four platforms can take hours. Multiply that by your entire catalog and you're looking at a massive time investment.

This is where AI-powered listing tools become essential. Instead of spending hours per product, you can generate platform-specific, SEO-optimized listings in seconds and focus your time on sourcing, marketing, and growing your business.

Final Thoughts: Diversify and Optimize

The e-commerce landscape in 2026 rewards sellers who diversify across platforms while keeping every listing optimized for its specific marketplace. Amazon gives you volume, Etsy gives you community, Shopify gives you ownership, and eBay gives you flexibility.

The smartest approach is to start where your products fit best, build momentum, and then expand to additional platforms as you grow. Wherever you sell, make sure your product listings are fully optimized for each platform's unique requirements — that's what separates top sellers from everyone else.
