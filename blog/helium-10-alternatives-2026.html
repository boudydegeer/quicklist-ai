Why Sellers Are Looking Beyond Helium 10 in 2026

Helium 10 has been the go-to Amazon seller suite for years, and for good reason. It built one of the deepest keyword databases in the industry and offered tools for nearly every aspect of selling on Amazon. But in 2026, a growing number of sellers are questioning whether it's still worth the cost.

Helium 10 raised prices again. Platinum now sits at $99/mo (up from $79 just two years ago), and Diamond jumped to $279/mo. For a tool that exclusively serves Amazon, that's a steep ask — especially when most sellers only use three or four tools out of the 30+ included in the suite.

Then there's the platform problem. If you sell on Amazon and Etsy, or Amazon and Shopify, Helium 10 doesn't help with the second marketplace. You're paying premium prices for Amazon-only functionality while the rest of your business goes unoptimized.

Meanwhile, a new generation of AI-powered tools has emerged that can do listing optimization — arguably the most common use case for Helium 10 — better, faster, and cheaper. The "$99/month for keyword research" era is coming to an end, and sellers are starting to notice.

What Helium 10 Does Well (And Where It Falls Short)

Let's be fair: Helium 10 isn't a bad tool. It's one of the most feature-rich platforms in the Amazon seller ecosystem, and for certain sellers, it's still the right choice.

Strengths

  • Deepest Amazon keyword database. Cerebro reverse ASIN lookup is still best-in-class for competitive keyword research.
  • Comprehensive suite. Product research, keyword tracking, listing optimization, PPC management, inventory protection, review monitoring — it's all there.
  • Established community. Years of tutorials, courses, and a large user base mean you can find help for almost any question.
  • Regular updates. Helium 10 keeps pace with Amazon algorithm changes faster than most competitors.

Where It Falls Short

  • Price-to-value ratio for small sellers. Most sellers use Cerebro, Magnet, and the Listing Builder. That's three tools out of 30+. You're paying for a lot of features you'll never touch.
  • Amazon-only. Zero support for Etsy, Shopify, eBay, or any other marketplace. In a multi-channel world, this is a growing limitation.
  • AI listing builder is decent but not best-in-class. It gets the job done, but newer AI-native tools produce more natural, higher-converting copy.
  • Steep learning curve. New sellers often spend weeks just figuring out which tools to use and how they fit together.
  • Feature bloat. Many tools overlap in functionality, creating confusion rather than clarity.

Jungle Scout — The Most Direct Competitor

Price: ~$49/mo

Jungle Scout is probably the first name that comes to mind when sellers think about Helium 10 alternatives. It's been around almost as long, has a loyal user base, and offers a cleaner experience at roughly half the price.

The platform includes product research, keyword research, a listing builder, sales analytics, and a solid browser extension for on-the-fly product evaluation. Their AI Assist feature generates first-draft listings that, while not perfect, give you a strong starting point.

Pros

  • 50% cheaper than Helium 10 Platinum at $49/mo
  • Cleaner, more intuitive interface — less time learning, more time selling
  • Strong product research with the Opportunity Finder
  • Established community with solid educational resources
  • Browser extension is one of the best for quick product evaluation

Cons

  • Still Amazon-only — no multi-marketplace support
  • AI features are improving but less advanced than dedicated AI listing tools
  • Reverse ASIN capabilities don't match Cerebro's depth
  • Single pricing tier means no budget entry point for beginners
  • Keyword database, while good, is slightly smaller than Helium 10's

Best For

Amazon sellers who want most of what Helium 10 offers at a more reasonable price point, with a gentler learning curve.

SellerApp — The Underrated All-Rounder

Price: ~$39/mo

SellerApp doesn't get the same press as Jungle Scout or Helium 10, but it's quietly built a solid platform that punches above its weight. At roughly $39/mo, it's the most affordable full-featured alternative on this list.

The platform covers keyword research, listing optimization, PPC analytics, and a profit dashboard that gives you a clear picture of your margins. They've also expanded to support Flipkart, making them one of the few tools with any kind of multi-marketplace functionality.

Pros

  • Most affordable full-featured option at ~$39/mo — 61% cheaper than Helium 10 Platinum
  • Built-in PPC management saves you from buying a separate ad tool
  • Clean, modern dashboard that's easy to navigate
  • Expanding marketplace support (Amazon + Flipkart)
  • Profit dashboard gives real margin visibility, not just revenue numbers

Cons

  • Smaller keyword database compared to Helium 10 and Jungle Scout
  • Less community support and educational resources
  • Flipkart support is niche — not useful for US or EU sellers
  • No Etsy, Shopify, or eBay support
  • Brand recognition is lower, which shouldn't matter but sometimes affects trust

Best For

Budget-conscious Amazon sellers who want a capable all-in-one tool without the premium price tag, especially those also selling on Flipkart.

ZonGuru — The Product Research Specialist

Price: ~$49/mo

ZonGuru has carved out a niche as the product research specialist in the Amazon seller tool space. If your primary challenge is finding the right products to sell rather than optimizing existing listings, ZonGuru deserves a serious look.

Their standout features include Keywords on Fire (trending keyword discovery), Niche Finder (market opportunity analysis), and a unique Love-Hate analysis that mines customer reviews to identify what buyers actually care about. They also offer a Listing Optimizer, though it's not their strongest feature.

Pros

  • Excellent product research tools — some of the best in the category
  • Love-Hate review analysis is unique and genuinely insightful
  • Clean, modern UI that's pleasant to work with
  • Keywords on Fire helps you spot trends before competitors
  • Solid keyword research tools for everyday optimization

Cons

  • Amazon-only, like most tools in this space
  • Similar price to Jungle Scout but with a smaller user base
  • Fewer educational resources and community support
  • Listing optimization is functional but not its core strength
  • Less robust PPC features compared to SellerApp or Helium 10

Best For

Amazon sellers in the product research phase, or established sellers looking for data-driven insights into new product opportunities.

Catalister — The AI Newcomer

Price: Credit-based (pay per listing)

Catalister takes a fundamentally different approach to pricing. Instead of a monthly subscription, you buy credits and pay per listing generated. For sellers who only need to create or optimize a handful of listings per month, this can be dramatically cheaper than any subscription tool.

The platform focuses squarely on AI-generated listings with SEO optimization baked in. You provide your product details, and Catalister's AI produces a complete, optimized listing. It's a modern, focused tool that does one thing and does it reasonably well.

Pros

  • Pay-per-use model is cost-effective for low-volume sellers
  • No monthly commitment — only pay when you need a listing
  • Modern AI produces clean, readable copy
  • Simple workflow with no learning curve
  • Good for sellers who launch a few products at a time rather than dozens

Cons

  • Newer tool with a shorter track record — less proven at scale
  • Credit system can get expensive quickly if you're optimizing many listings
  • Limited analytics and no ongoing optimization insights
  • Primarily Amazon-focused
  • No product research, keyword tracking, or PPC management

Best For

Low-volume sellers who need occasional listing optimization without committing to a monthly subscription.
