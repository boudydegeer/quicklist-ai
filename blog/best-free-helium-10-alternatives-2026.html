Best Free Helium 10 Alternatives in 2026: Tools That Won't Break the Bank

By QuickList AI Team · March 2026

TL;DR: Helium 10 is powerful but expensive, starting at $29/month for a limited Starter plan and $79/month for anything useful. If you are looking for a free Helium 10 alternative in 2026, your best options are QuickList AI (free tier with AI listing generation across Amazon, Etsy, and Shopify), SellerApp (freemium with basic analytics), and Keywords Everywhere (pay-per-credit keyword data). Why Sellers Are Looking for Helium 10 Alternatives in 2026

Helium 10 has been the go-to Amazon seller toolkit for years, and for good reason. It offers keyword research, listing optimization, product tracking, profitability calculators, and more. But there is one problem that keeps coming up in seller forums and Reddit threads: the price.

As of early 2026, Helium 10's pricing looks like this:

  • Free plan: Extremely limited. You get a handful of searches per day and basic access to a few tools. Not enough to run a real business on.
  • Starter ($29/mo): Still limited. Caps on keyword searches and no access to advanced features like Cerebro's full dataset or Adtomic.
  • Platinum ($79/mo): Where it starts getting useful, but that is nearly $950 per year.
  • Diamond ($229/mo): Full access, but this is enterprise-level pricing that most solo sellers and small businesses cannot justify.

For new sellers, side hustlers, or anyone selling on multiple marketplaces (not just Amazon), paying $79 to $229 per month for a tool that is primarily Amazon-focused feels like overkill. That is exactly why the search for a free Helium 10 alternative has exploded in 2026.

Let us walk through the best options available right now, with honest assessments of what each tool does well and where it falls short.

2. Jungle Scout — Best for Product Research (Limited Free Access)

Free tier: Very limited (Opportunity Finder only, few searches)
Paid plan: Starting at $49/month
Marketplaces: Amazon only

Jungle Scout is Helium 10's most direct competitor and has been around just as long. In 2026, they offer a very limited free experience through their Opportunity Finder tool, which lets you explore product niches with a handful of free searches per day.

What it does well:

  • Excellent product research and sales estimation data
  • Supplier Database is unique and genuinely useful for sourcing
  • AccuSales algorithm is considered one of the most accurate for sales estimates
  • Clean, intuitive interface that is easier to learn than Helium 10

Where it falls short:

  • Free tier is barely functional — you will hit limits within minutes
  • Paid plans are also expensive ($49-$84/month)
  • Amazon-only. No Etsy, Shopify, or eBay support
  • Listing builder exists but lacks AI generation capabilities

Jungle Scout is a great tool if you are committed to Amazon FBA and have the budget. As a free Helium 10 alternative, though, the free tier is more of a teaser than a tool you can rely on daily.

Free tier: Yes, freemium with basic analytics

Marketplaces: Amazon, Flipkart

SellerApp offers one of the more generous free tiers among Amazon seller tools. Their free plan includes a product research dashboard, a basic keyword tool, and listing quality scores. It is not unlimited, but it gives you enough to evaluate products and audit your existing listings.

What it does well:

  • Free tier includes actual analytics, not just a login wall

Where it falls short:

  • Data accuracy can be inconsistent, especially for lower-volume keywords
  • UI feels dated compared to Helium 10 or Jungle Scout
  • Primarily Amazon-focused (Flipkart support is niche)
  • Advanced features locked behind higher-priced plans

SellerApp is a solid choice if you want a free analytics dashboard to complement another listing tool. Pairing SellerApp's free analytics with QuickList AI's free listing generator, for example, covers a lot of ground without spending anything.

4. Keywords Everywhere — Best Budget Keyword Research

Free tier: No (credit-based, starts at $1.25 for 100,000 credits)
Paid plan: Pay-per-credit, extremely affordable
Marketplaces: Amazon, Google, YouTube, Etsy, eBay, and more

Keywords Everywhere is not a free tool, but it deserves a spot on this list because it is absurdly cheap. For $1.25, you get 100,000 keyword credits — which is enough for most sellers to last months. It works as a browser extension that shows keyword volume, CPC, and competition data directly on Amazon search pages, Google, Etsy, and other platforms.

What it does well:

  • Incredibly affordable — practically free for casual use
  • Shows keyword data directly in search results (no separate dashboard needed)
  • Works across multiple marketplaces and search engines
  • Trend data and related keyword suggestions are genuinely useful
  • Lightweight — does not slow down your browser

Where it falls short:

  • Keyword-only tool — no listing optimization, no product research, no analytics
  • Amazon-specific data is less detailed than Helium 10's Cerebro or Magnet
  • No listing builder or AI features
  • You still need other tools to act on the keyword data

Keywords Everywhere is the perfect companion tool. Use it for keyword research, then feed those keywords into QuickList AI's generator to create optimized listings. Total cost: under $2/month for most sellers.

5. ZonGuru — Best for Amazon Niche Research (Limited Free)

Paid plan: Starting at $49/month
Marketplaces: Amazon only

ZonGuru positions itself as an "all-in-one" Amazon toolkit, and it does pack a lot of features. Their free tier gives you limited access to the Niche Finder, Keywords on Fire, and Love/Hate analysis tools. It is enough to explore the platform and run a few product research sessions per day.

What it does well:

Where it falls short:

  • Free tier is heavily restricted — most tools cap at 2-3 uses per day
  • Paid plans are on the expensive side for what you get
  • Amazon-only, no multi-marketplace support
  • Listing optimizer is basic compared to AI-powered alternatives

ZonGuru is worth trying if you are specifically doing product research on Amazon. The Love/Hate review analysis can genuinely help you find product improvement opportunities. But as a daily driver replacement for Helium 10, the free tier is too limited.

6. SmartScout — Best for Brand and Category Analytics

Free tier: Yes, limited (brand and subcategory research)
Paid plan: Starting at $25/month
Marketplaces: Amazon only

SmartScout takes a different angle on Amazon research by focusing on brand-level and category-level analytics. Their free tier lets you explore brand market share, subcategory revenue estimates, and seller maps. It is a unique dataset that you will not find in Helium 10 or Jungle Scout.

What it does well:

  • Brand and subcategory data is genuinely unique among seller tools
  • UPC scanner and FBA calculator are included on free tier
  • Competitive pricing — $25/month for the basic plan is reasonable
  • Traffic graph shows where Amazon sends organic traffic within categories

Where it falls short:

  • No listing optimization or AI generation features
  • Free tier is useful for browsing but not for daily workflow
  • Amazon-only
  • Keyword research is not its strength

SmartScout fills a niche that other tools do not cover well: understanding the competitive landscape at a brand and category level. It pairs well with a listing tool like QuickList AI if you want research plus optimization.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a completely free version of Helium 10?

Helium 10 offers a free plan, but it is extremely limited. You get a small number of searches per day across their tools, and most advanced features (full keyword data, Cerebro reverse ASIN lookup, Profits dashboard) are locked behind paid plans. For most sellers, the free plan is useful for testing the interface but not for running a business.

Can free tools really replace Helium 10?

It depends on what features you use. If you primarily use Helium 10 for listing optimization, a tool like QuickList AI can replace that functionality on its free tier. If you rely heavily on Cerebro for reverse ASIN lookups or Black Box for product research, you will need to piece together multiple free tools or accept some limitations.

What is the cheapest way to optimize Amazon listings in 2026?

The cheapest effective approach is using QuickList AI's free tier for AI-generated listings combined with Keywords Everywhere for keyword research. This gives you optimized listings with the right keywords for under $2/month total.

Are these alternatives good for beginners?

Yes. In fact, tools like QuickList AI are arguably better for beginners than Helium 10 because they are simpler and more focused. Helium 10's learning curve is steep — there are dozens of tools, and knowing which ones to use and how to interpret the data takes time. A focused listing tool gets you to results faster.
